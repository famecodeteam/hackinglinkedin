# Your Voice Guide

**This is the most important file in the repo.** It's the single source of truth for how *your* posts sound. The writer reads it (plus `voice-examples.md`) before every draft. A vague guide gives vague drafts - spend real time here.

Replace everything below with your own voice. The prompts show you what to fill in.

---

## The one-sentence version

> In one line, how does your writing sound? (e.g. "Blunt, contrarian one-liner as the opening. One idea per line. Real numbers over adjectives. A founder talking to a friend, never presenting at a conference.")

---

## Voice rules

**Hook - how you open.**
> What does your first line do? Paste 3-5 real opening lines you've used that worked. The first sentence is the whole bet - describe the shape of a great one for you.

**Structure.**
> How is your text laid out? (line breaks, paragraph length, use of lists, white space)

**Proof / specifics.**
> What makes a claim credible in your writing? (real numbers, named examples, screenshots) List a few real data points you reach for.

**Enemy framing (optional).**
> Do your posts argue against something? List the "enemies" you write against (e.g. a bad practice, a piece of conventional wisdom).

**Tone.**
> Dry? Warm? Provocative? Self-deprecating? How do you handle a win (brag vs undercut)?

**Formatting rules.**
> Emoji (how much?), hashtags (yes/no?), anything you never do.

**Length.**
> Your target word count and rhythm.

**Close.**
> How do your posts end? (a CTA, a callback, nothing, a question)

## Anti-patterns (never do these)

> List the things that would make a post NOT sound like you (buzzwords, throat-clearing intros, engagement bait, etc.).
